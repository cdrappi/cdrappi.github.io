Summary of: On the Donation of Constantine by Lorenzo Valla

tl;dr

constantine (272-337) was the first christian emperor. as the roman empire fell, the church gained influence and territory. around 760, after they had enough power to do so, they forged a document known as the Donation of Constantine, where the emperor signs away full control of the western roman empire to the church because the pope cured him of leprosy

in 1440, an italian priest named LORENZO VALLA analyzed the donation and proved that it was fake. his arguments were:

  • logic
    : there's no way constantine would do this or rome would let him do this. if he did, the romans would have murdered all of the priests since most people weren't on board with christianity yet
  • linguistic
    : the document's latin was so shitty, there's no way it was written by an emperor. also, it used words that romans wouldn't have used at the time
  • medical
    : you don't become a leper overnight
  • moral
    : the bible preaches doing good things for no rewards. a good pope wouldn't have accepted half of an empire for curing leprocy
  • contextual
    : the document was written on a piece of paper rather than a bronze plate or something more permanent. who would sign away the western roman empire on a napkin?

valla's wikipedia page only goes into the logical argument and briefly mentions the anachronism but to me the other points are just as convincing

HISTORICAL CONTEXT

  • Before Constantine (272-337), the citizens of the Roman Empire were pagan, following the ancient Roman religion -- basically a knock-off of the ancient Greek religion.
  • In 313, Constantine declared that it was okay to be a Christian. He was warming up to Christianity himself
  • In 325, Constantine gathered up a bunch of Christian bishops and they defined what it meant to believe in Christianity
  • Although he proclaimed to believe in Christianity as a forty-something, Constantine waited until his death in 337 to be baptized. He thought that if he waited, God would forgive all of the sins he'd commit during his reign. Genius
  • Pope Sylvester I (?-335) became Pope in 314 until his death
  • As the church got more extreme, they decided it wasn't okay that Constantine was baptized on his deathbed. So, the church made up a legend:
